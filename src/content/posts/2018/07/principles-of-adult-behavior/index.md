<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><em>By John Perry Barlow, 1977</em></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>1  </strong> Be patient. No matter what.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>2  </strong> Don’t badmouth: Assign responsibility, not blame. Say nothing of another you wouldn’t say to him in the same language and tone of voice.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>3</strong>   Never assume the motives of others are, to them, less noble than yours are to you.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>4</strong>   Expand your sense of the possible.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>5</strong>   Don’t trouble yourself with matters you truly cannot change.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>6</strong>   Expect no more of anyone than you can deliver yourself.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>7</strong>   Tolerate ambiguity.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>8</strong>   Laugh at yourself frequently.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>9</strong>   Concern yourself with what is right rather than who is right.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>10</strong>  Never forget that, no matter how certain, you might be wrong.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>11 </strong> Give up blood sports.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>12</strong>  Remember that your life belongs to others as well. Don’t risk it frivolously.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>13</strong>  Never lie to anyone for any reason. (Lies of omission are sometimes exempt.)</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>14</strong>  Learn the needs of those around you and respect them.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>15</strong>  Avoid the pursuit of happiness. Seek to define your mission and pursue that.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>16</strong>  Reduce your use of the first personal pronoun.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>17</strong>  Praise at least as often as you disparage.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>18</strong>  Admit your errors freely and soon.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>19 </strong> Become less suspicious of joy.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>20</strong>  Understand humility.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>21</strong>  Remember that love forgives everything.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>22</strong>  Foster dignity.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>23 </strong> Live memorably.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>24</strong>  Love yourself.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>25</strong>  Endure.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->
