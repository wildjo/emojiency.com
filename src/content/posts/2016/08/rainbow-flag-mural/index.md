Photoshop's Free Transform tool is completely priceless.



Let me not say priceless, because I have been less than excited <img class=" wp-image-382 alignleft" src="http://emojiency.com/bloggityblogblog/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/IMG_1087-300x225.jpg" alt="LightRailLeft" width="311" height="233" />about the idea of paying twenty bucks a month to have access to it. I'm only slightly less disgusted to think of spending a mere tenner on the new moon for the privilege.

It really has no equal in the image editing arena. Stitching together multiple photos, taken from different angles, becomes almost s<img class="size-medium wp-image-372 alignright" src="http://emojiency.com/bloggityblogblog/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/IMG_1086-300x225.jpg" alt="LightRailRight" width="300" height="225" />imple. Take these two examples. I was too close to get the whole building in one shot, so I swiveled and got the rest in a second shot. This meant the two had been taken from different angles, something that could be simply repaired by a free transform.

Then the flag gets added as a Color Dodge, with some brush strokes added for perspective and to dirty up the paint a bit.

<img class="alignnone size-large wp-image-402" src="http://emojiency.com/bloggityblogblog/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/Screen-Shot-2016-08-03-at-3.17.38-PM-1024x750.png" alt="Free Transform" width="474" height="347" />

I'm not completely happy with it, but it was a good morning's work. I think if I were to spend more time, I would have desaturated the yellow stripe a bit to make it stand out less.

Next time I think I'll add a cement pump to a reservoir.
