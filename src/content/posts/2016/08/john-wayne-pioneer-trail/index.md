Logo design for the John Wayne Pioneer Trail in the Iron Horse State Park.



<img class="alignnone size-large wp-image-1852" src="http://emojiency.com/bloggityblogblog/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/IMG_1262-1024x576.jpg" alt="IMG_1262" width="474" height="267" />

The idea hit me while I was preparing for a bikepacking trip from Seattle to Ellensburg and back. I sketched it out quick in <a href="http://procreate.si/pocket/">Procreate Pocket</a>.

Finally got around to purchasing <a href="https://affinity.serif.com/">Affinity Designer</a> after the trial expired. I like it quite a bit, and it works fabulously with the Apple Pencil, iPad Pro and <a href="http://astropad.com">Astropad</a>.

<img class="alignnone size-large wp-image-1832" src="http://emojiency.com/bloggityblogblog/wp-content/uploads/2016/08/Screen-Shot-2016-08-31-at-11.54.34-AM-1024x475.png" alt="Screen Shot 2016-08-31 at 11.54.34 AM" width="474" height="220" />

The bike rider icon came pretty quickly, as it is the standard bike lane logo used by the departments of transportation around here. Translating the bike lane symbol into a covered wagon symbol was way more difficult than I thought it would be, but I think it turned out pretty well in the final version.

I love the icon aesthetic of creating images that quickly convey ideas using simple lines or pixels.
